Peace Commissioner FAQ

Frequently asked questions about Peace Commissioner services

What is a Peace Commissioner?

A Peace Commissioner is a person appointed by the Minister for Justice to provide certain services to the public, including witnessing signatures on documents, taking statutory declarations, and certifying copies of documents. Peace Commissioners act as an important public service to help with various legal and administrative matters.

What documents can a Peace Commissioner witness or certify?

Peace Commissioners can witness or certify various documents including:

  • Statutory declarations and affidavits
  • Passport application forms
  • Certified copies of original documents
  • Marriage notice forms
  • Proof of identity documents
  • Various legal and official forms

Do I need an appointment to see a Peace Commissioner?

Yes, it is recommended to book an appointment in advance to ensure availability and to minimize waiting times. You can book an appointment through the contact page on this website or by calling directly. Some urgent cases may be accommodated without an appointment, but advance booking is preferred.

What should I bring to my appointment?

You should bring:

  • The document(s) you need witnessed or certified
  • Original identification (passport, driver's license, etc.)
  • Any supporting documents mentioned in the forms
  • Payment for any applicable fees (if required)
  • Completed forms (if possible) to save time

Is there a fee for Peace Commissioner services?

Peace Commissioner services are generally provided free of charge for personal matters. However, some commissioners may charge a small fee for business or commercial services, or for extensive document preparation. Any applicable fees will be clearly communicated in advance of the appointment.

Can a Peace Commissioner provide legal advice?

No, Peace Commissioners cannot provide legal advice. Their role is to witness signatures, take declarations, and certify documents. For legal advice, you should consult a solicitor or legal professional. A Peace Commissioner can help with the proper execution of documents but cannot advise on legal matters.

How long does the process take?

Most Peace Commissioner services take only 10-15 minutes per document, assuming all paperwork is in order and proper identification is provided. Complex cases or multiple documents may take longer. It's best to arrive prepared with all necessary documents to ensure a smooth and efficient process.

Can documents be certified remotely or online?

Most Peace Commissioner services require in-person attendance as the commissioner needs to verify your identity and witness you signing the documents. However, some limited services may be available remotely in certain circumstances. Please inquire when booking your appointment if you have specific needs regarding remote services.
